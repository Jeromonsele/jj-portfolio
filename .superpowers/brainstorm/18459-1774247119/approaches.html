3 Approaches for the Portfolio Redesign

Each takes the same raw material but structures it differently. Click the one that feels right.

A

The PM Portfolio (Pure Edit)

Keep the current single-page site. Ruthlessly re-edit it for PM signal.


What changes:

  • Hero rewritten: outcomes + positioning line
  • 3 proof chips replace credibility bar (1.87M properties, 80% AI adoption, $100K Nasher Prize)
  • Nav becomes: About / Case Studies / Products / Writing / Contact
  • 3 flagship case studies replace 21 equal-weight projects
  • Case study cards with Problem → Decision → Outcome format
  • Everything else demoted to "Archive" or removed
  • "Coming soon" articles hidden until real
  • Story/Principles/Journey condensed into a short "About" section

Pros
  • Fastest to ship (1-2 days)
  • Existing aesthetic preserved
  • Single page = recruiter scans in 30 seconds
Cons
  • Case studies live on the homepage as cards, not full pages
  • Limited depth — no room for the full decision narrative
  • Doesn't match the "world-class" visual standard
B

The Case Study Portfolio (Full Redesign)

New homepage + 3 dedicated case study pages. The site becomes a PM portfolio, not a personal site.


What changes:

  • New hero: positioning line + 3 proof metrics + headshot
  • Nav: About / Case Studies / Products / Writing / Contact
  • Homepage shows 3 case study cards (image + problem + outcome + "Read Case Study →")
  • Each case study gets a full page: Problem → Insight → Options → Decision → Metrics → Reflection
  • Dedicated /about page with condensed story, principles, credentials
  • Products section: live links to SearchFundDB, TVGHC, Diagnostic
  • Writing section: only published pieces (TEDx, newsletter)
  • Archive page for builder proof and lab experiments

Pros
  • Full depth on each case study — shows your thinking
  • Matches what PM hiring managers expect in 2026
  • Room for metrics, decisions, trade-offs, reflections
  • Can share individual case study URLs with recruiters
Cons
  • More work (3-4 days)
  • Requires writing 3 full case study narratives
  • Need to extract decision details from your memory
C

The Hybrid (Recommended)

New homepage designed for PM signal + 3 case study pages + keep the best of the current site.


What changes:

  • New hero: "Product & operations leader..." + 3 proof chips + CTA
  • Nav: Case Studies / Products / About / Writing / Contact
  • Homepage: Hero → Proof chips → 3 Case Study cards → Live Products strip → Short bio → CTA
  • 3 full case study pages (ASG, Nasher, TVGHC)
  • /about page: condensed story + principles (best of current site) + credentials
  • /products page: SearchFundDB, TVGHC estimator, 4 Gaps Diagnostic, AskJJ — live products that prove you build
  • /writing page: TEDx + newsletter + published articles only
  • /archive page: builder proof + lab (preserved, just not front-and-center)
  • Visual polish: higher contrast, tighter typography, reduced motion, better spacing
  • SEO: metadata, OG images, sitemap, JSON-LD
  • Accessibility: semantic HTML, focus states, contrast, reduced-motion

Pros
  • PM-optimized but keeps operator identity
  • Full case study depth + shareable URLs
  • Products page is rare differentiator — most PMs can't show live shipped products
  • Nothing good gets deleted, just reorganized
  • Meets the visual + SEO + accessibility bar
Cons
  • Most work (4-6 days)
  • Still requires writing 3 case study narratives
  • More pages to maintain

My Recommendation

Option C (Hybrid) — because the Products page is your secret weapon. Most PM candidates show case studies about products they managed at other companies. You can show live, working products you built yourself. That's a moat. The case study pages prove you think like a PM. The products page proves you ship like a founder. Together, that's the "product-minded operator" signal you want.
